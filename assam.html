Assam

The land of Bihu, tea gardens, and the mighty Brahmaputra.

← Back to States

Festivals

Bihu is Assam’s most celebrated festival, marking the Assamese New Year with dance, music, and feasts. Ambubachi Mela at Kamakhya Temple and Majuli Raas are also major cultural events.

Traditional Arts & Crafts

Assam is famous for its Jaapi (bamboo hat), Bell metal crafts, and Assamese silk (Muga, Eri, Pat). The state’s artisans are renowned for their skill in weaving and metalwork.

Folk Dances

Bihu dance is the most popular folk dance, performed during the Bihu festival. Sattriya is a classical dance form originating from Assam’s Vaishnavite monasteries.

Handicrafts & Textiles

Assam is renowned for Muga silk and Eri silk textiles, as well as bamboo and cane products. Sualkuchi is known as the “Manchester of Assam” for its silk weaving.

Unique Facts

Assam is home to Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for the one-horned rhinoceros. The Brahmaputra is one of the few rivers in the world with a male name.
